Chapter 10 Graphs

10.1 Graph and Graph Models

Basic Terminology

DEFENITION 1
A graph(图) G = (V,E) consists of V, a nonempty set of vertices (or nodes) and E, a set of edges(边). Each edge has either one or two vertices associated with it, called endpoints(端点). An edge is said to connect(联结) its endpoints.

A graph with an infinite vertex set or an infinite number of edges is called an infinite graph(无限图).

A graph with a finite vertex set and a finite edge set is called a finite graph(有限图).

A graph in which each edge connects two different vertices and where no two edges connect the same pair of vertices is called a simple graph(简单图).

10.2 Graph Terminology and Special Types of Graphs

DEFINITION 1
Two verticies u and v in an undirected graph G are called adjacent(相邻的) (or neighbors(邻居)) in G if u and v are endpoints of an edge e of G. Such an edge e is called incident with(关联) the vertices u and v and e is said to connect(联结) u and v.

DEFENITION 2
The set of all neighbors of a vertex v of G = (V,E), denoted by N(v), is called the neighborhood(邻居) of v. If A is a subset of V, we denote by N(A) the set of all vertices in G that are adjacent to at least one vertex in A. So,
N(A) = N(v).

DEFINITION 3
The degree of a vertex(顶点的度) in an undirected graph(无向图) is the inmber of edges incident(关联) with it, except that a loop(环) at a vertex contributes twice to the degree of that vertex. The degree of the vertex v is denoted by deg(v).

A vertex of degree zero is called isolated(孤立的).

A vertex is pendant(悬挂的) if and only if it has degree one.

DEFINITION 4
When (u,v) is an edge of the graph G with directed edges, u is said to be adjacent to(与···相邻) v and v is said to be adjacent from(从···相邻) u. The vertex u is called the initial vertex(起点) of (u,v), and v is called the terminal(终点) or end vertex(终点) of (u,v). The initial vertex and terminal vertex of a loop are the same.

DEFINITION 5
In a graph with directed edges the in-degree(入度) of a vertex v,denoted by deg (v), is the number of edges with v as their terminal vertex.
The out-degree(出度) of v, denoted by deg (v), is the number of edges with v as their initial vertex.(Note that a loop at a vertex contributes 1 to both the in-degree and the out-degree of this vertex.)

10.3 Representing Graphs and Graph Isomorphism

Sometimes, two graphs have exactly the same form, in the sense that there is a one-to-one
correspondence between their vertex sets that preserves edges. In such a case, we say that the
two graphs are isomorphic(同构).

One way to represent a graph without multiple edges is to list all the edges of this graph. Another
way to represent a graph with no multiple edges is to use adjacency lists(邻接表), which specify the
vertices that are adjacent to each vertex of the graph.

The adjacency matrix(邻接矩阵) of a sparse graph(稀疏图) is a sparse matrix(稀疏矩阵).

DEFINITION 1
The simple graphs G1 = (V1, E1) and G2 = (V2, E2) are isomorphic if there exists a one-to-one and onto function f from to with the property that a and b are adjacent in if
and only if f(a) and f(b) are adjacent in , for all a and b in . Such a function f is called
an isomorphism(同构).∗ Two simple graphs that are not isomorphic are called nonisomorphic(非同构).

11.1 Introduction to Trees

DEFINITION 1
A tree(树) is a connected undirected graph with no simple circuits.

THEOREM 1
An undirected graph is a tree if and only if there is a unique simple path between any two of its vertices.

DEFINITION 2
A rooted tree(有根树) is a tree in which one vertex has been designated as the root and every edge is directed away from the root.

DEFINITION 3
A rooted tree is called an m-ary tree if every internal vertex has no more than m children. The tree is called a full m-ary tree if every internal vertex has exactly m children. An m-ary tree with is called a binary tree(二叉树).
