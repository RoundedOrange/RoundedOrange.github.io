山东大学软件学院2022年英语科技文献阅读与翻译
课本1-20章

外语教学与研究出版社-赵毓琴、常梅《英语科技文献阅读》
ISBN：978-7-5213-1225-6

关于我为什么要写这个笔记：这门课要记得点比较少，记下来可能以后能帮助学弟学妹，每年题型可能变化，具体依照各自老师。这篇笔记只画重点。

Introduction of scientific literature

  • According to the processing depth, scientific literature can be classified into:

    1. primary literature(第一类文献)
    2. secondary literature(第二类文献)

    3. tertiary literature(第三类文献)

      期末考要学会辨别属于哪一类文献
      |type|examples|
      |:—:|:—:|
      |Primary Literature|novel scientific research;conference documents;patents;technical reports;standards|
      |Secondary Literature|review articles;books|
      |Tertiary literature|textbooks;encyclopedias;similar works intended for broad public consumption|

  • Stylistic features
    • Formality
    • Conciseness
    • Directness
    • Objectivity
    • Use of non-verbal languange
  • technical and scientific jargon=TSJ
  • According to the degree of “technicalness”, TSJ can be divided into two categories: general TSJ and specific TSJ. The former has a much larger number than the latter.
    1. General TSJ: the words that can be used in different fields with different meanings.
    2. Specific TSJ: the words that are usually restricted to given fields.

Journals

  1. The purpose of a scientific journal is to give researchers a venue to impart their knowledge to one another, and make contributions to enriching the knowledge of all sciences.
    学术期刊的目标不包括帮科学家搞钱。
  2. Types of journal articles
    • Original research articles
    • Review articles(summarizes the current state of understanding of a topic. also called survey articles or overview articles)
    • Perspective, opinion or commentary articles
    • Book reviews
  3. Journal metrics
    • are intended to reflect the place of a journal within its field, the relative difficulty of an article published in that journal, and the pretige associated with it.
    • IF(Impact Factor) or JIF(Journal Impact Factor):the yearly average number of citations to recent articles published in that journal.
    • Eigenfactor
    • h-index:an author-level metric that attempts to measure both the productivity and citation impact of the publications of a scientist or scholar.
    • Immediacy Index(立即检索):the number of citations the articles in a journal receive in a given year divided by the number of articles published.
    • Cited Half-Life:the number of years, going back from the current year, that accounts for half the total citations received by the cited journal in the current year.
  4. Identification code
    • DOI
    • IDS Number(期刊编号)
    • ISSN(8位)
    • ISBN(国际标准书号，2007年前10位，之后13位)
    • UT ISI(文章唯一标识符)

Monographs

  1. Usually, only one author is involved, but the author may collaborate if necessary.
  2. Standards to measure if a book fits the description of a scholarly monograph.
    1. The author has an advanced degree, and/or is associated with an academic institution.
    2. The book is published by a university press, scholarly society, or other scholarly organizations.
    3. The book includes a bibliography at the end citing all of the sources that the author used in the research.
  3. Features of scholarly monograohs
    • Academicality
    • Creativity
    • Authoritativeness
    • Comprehensiveness
    • Systematicness
    • Beyond time and space

Conference documents

  1. Types of conference documents(According to conference stage)
    • Documents distributed before a conference
    • Documents distributed at a conference
    • Documents distributed after a conference
  2. Types of academic conferences
    • Conference
    • Forum
    • Lecture
    • Panel
    • Seminar
    • Symposium
    • Workshop

Graduation papers

  1. Types
    • According to the degree
      • the graduation paper for a bachelor’s degree
      • the graduation paper for a master’s degree
      • the graduation paper for a doctorate
    • According to the research methods
      • theoretical research paper
      • experimental research paper
      • descriptive research paper
    • According to the research field
      • the research paper in arts and humanities
      • the research paper in science and engineering
  2. Differences between a graduation paper and a journal article
    • In terms of purpose
      • A graduation paper is to justify that the student has reached a certain academic level in his or her specialty and can graduate with a certain level of academic degree.
      • A journal article is to indicate that the author has accomplished a research project and wants to share his or her research results with others.
    • In terms of length: graduation paper is much longer
    • In terms of abstract: the abstract of a graduation paper is longer
    • In terms of the Method section
      • in a graduation paper, student should describe Method section in an explicit way
      • Methods section of a journal article is often writte in a brief way
    • In terms of references: graduation paper lists more references
    • In terms of details
      • a graduation paper provides details in each section
      • a journal article focus on some selective details
    • In terms of the number of authors
      • a graduation paper is writtem by only one author
      • a journal article can be co-written by more than one author

Technical reports

  1. Types
    • In terms of content
      • research findings reports
      • technical information reports
      • technical specifications reports
      • technical evaluation reports
      • technical recommendation reports
      • technical manuals
      • instructions reports
    • In terms of research progress
      • primary reports
      • progressive reports
      • interim reports
      • final reports
    • In terms of the scope of circulation
      • top secret reports
      • secret reports
      • confidential reports
      • restricted reports
      • unclassified reports
      • declassified reports
    • In terms of formality
      • informal reports
      • semifornal reports
      • formal reports
  2. Four series of technical reports in America
    • AD reports
    • PB reports
    • NASA reports
    • DOE reports

Science news

  1. Science news has the nature of both news and science.
  2. Science news is explicit, precise, and tries not to include jargon.
  3. Types
    • science news stories
    • science newsletters
    • science feature stories
    • science news commentaries

Patents

  1. It is granted by the government to the inventor for a period of usually 20 years from the filing date of a patent application.
  2. Usually, a patent application consists of the following elements
    • a request
    • a description
    • claims
    • drawings(if necessary)
    • a summary
  3. Types(according to stipulations of patent laws in many countries)
    • inventions
    • utility models
    • designs
  4. Typically, the duration of a copyright spans the author's life plus 50 to 100 years(that is, a copyright typically expires 50 t0 100 years after the author dies, depending on the jurisdiction).
    In the case of joint works, copyright protection is granted for the length of the life of the last surviving joint creator plus another 70 years.
    Works made for hire, as well as anonymous and pseudonymous works, are protected for a term of either 95 years from the year of first publication or 120 years from the year of creation, whichever is shorter.

Product specifications

  1. Features
    • genuine
    • scientific
    • logical
    • practical
    • directional
  2. Types(in terms of fields)
    • industrial product specifications
    • agricultural product specifications
    • financial product specifications
    • insurance specifications

Government publications

  1. Types
    • The administrative type
    • The scientific type
  2. International government publications(IGP)
    • UN publications
    • OECD publications
  3. American governemnt publications
    • GPO publications
    • GPO monthly catalog
    • NTIS publications

Standards

  1. Types
    • in terms of applicable scope
      • international standards
      • regional standards
      • national standards
      • professional standards
      • institutional standards
    • in terms of content
      • basic standards
      • manufacturing standards
      • standards of methods
  2. ISO is the International Organization for Standardization that sets standards in many businesses and technologies, including computing and communications.
  3. ISO 9000 can be defined as a set of international standards on quality management and quality assurance developed to help companies effectively document the quality elements needed to maintain an efficient quality system.

Title and author affiliation

  1. The title of a research article is usually characterized by
    • using more nonus or noun phrases, or gerunds
    • being brief and concise
    • being specific
    • using more compounds
  2. Types of titles
    • declarative titles
    • descriptive titles
    • interrogative titles（问句）
    • Compound titles（有主标题副标题）
  3. 排第一的是第一作者，带星标的是通信作者

Abstract

  1. An abstract usually consists of five parts
    • background
    • problem
    • methods
    • results
    • conclusion
  2. Types
    • informative abstracts
    • indicative abstracts
    • structured abstracts
    • graphical abstracts

Introduction

  1. Move
    • Establishing a territory
    • Reviewing previous related research
    • Establishing a nich
    • Occupying the niche

Methods

  1. The section includes
    • materials
    • instruments
    • subjects
    • procedures
    • data collection
    • data analysis
  2. Types of research
    • Based on methods
      • non-experimental research
      • experimental research
      • quasi-experimental research
    • Based on originality
      • primary research
      • secondary research

  3. The following kinds of research require ethical approval before the research begins

    • research involving living human subjects
    • research involving human remains, cadavers, tissuse, biological fluids, embryos, etc.
    • research that involves gathering information about human beings and organizations through interviewing, surveying, questionnaires, observation of human behavior, audio/video taping, and administering tests or stimuli

    • research involving secondary use of data——health records, employe records, student records, computer listings, banked tissues

      简单记为

    • 活人
    • 死人
    • 个人和公司机构隐私
    • 二手数据的使用

Results

  1. The Result section of a research article is to present research findings along with illustrations in the form of figures and tables.
  2. Features
    • Be objective
    • Be concise
    • Use non-verbal language
    • Use mixed tenses
  3. Moves
    • Providing preparatory information
    • Describing the data in figures and tables
    • Reporting the results based on the data

Discussion

  1. Features
    • It is organized from the specific to the general.
    • It has the same point of view as that in the Introduction section.
    • There are facts as well as opinions or comments in this section.
  2. Moves
    • reviewing the present study
    • consolidating the results
      • Step 1: Reporting the results
      • Step 2: Explaining the meaning of the findings and why they are important
      • Step 3: Relating the results to those of similar studies
      • Step 4: Providing alternative explanations for the results
    • stating limitations and possible future research directions

Conclusion

  1. Usually Conclusion contains the following information
    • restatement of the research and thesis statement
    • summary of the main points in the article
    • discussion of the significance of the research
    • presentation of the future of the subject
  2. Types
    • Thesis-oriented conclusion
      关注理论。
      typical structure:
      • Introductory statement
      • Consolidation of the research space
      • Recommendations
    • Field-oriented conclusion
      关注领域整体。

Acknowledgements

  1. features
    • Polite
    • Brief
    • Specific

References

  1. types of references
    • in-text references(in-text citation)
      可以直接引用也可以间接引用
    • end references
  2. styles
    要会分辨是三种中的哪种引用
    • APA style
      姓和年代
    • MLA style
      姓和页码
    • IEEE style
      有1、2、3、4标号
